I’m a freelance cartographer with a B.S. in Physical Geography from Montana State, three years as a Cartographic Technician at Grand Teton National Park, and 2,600 miles on the Pacific Crest Trail under my boots. I’ve been teaching myself GIS and selling original map drawings since I was fifteen — and I’ve never stopped.

Today I create maps full-time for clients who want something more than a template: photorealistic 3D terrain rendered in Blender, hand-drawn watercolor and pen & ink work, and rigorous GIS analysis for landscapes that deserve to be seen properly. Based in Jackson, Wyoming, where the mountains are never far from view.
