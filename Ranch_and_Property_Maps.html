Custom Ranch & Property Maps

Where Landscape Becomes Art

A custom map transforms your property into a work of art, equal parts accurate, personal, and timeless. Every map is designed to reflect how your land feels, not just how it looks.​ From small homesteads to expansive ranches and estates, every map is made to be treasured for generations.

Why commission a custom map:

  • Orient guests to roads, trails, and key landmarks
  • Showcase the property’s beauty and character through hand-painted detail
  • Create a lasting visual record of your land’s layout and legacy
  • Highlight favorite places: fishing holes, vistas, wildlife habitat, orchards, structures, trails and more
  • Commission an artwork worthy of display in your home or lodge
  • Gain new insights about your land for management, water, solar, or migration patterns through high resolution elevation data which can be provided in addition to an art piece

Every map I create begins with understanding your land, its history, its rhythms, and what makes it special to you. From field research and 3D modeling to sketching and painting, the process is collaborative from start to finish.

HOW IT WORKS

  1. Consultation & Planning: We discuss your goals, vision, and preferred map style. I'll send a quote.
  2. Mapping & Research: I gather terrain data, create 3D models, and reference imagery.
  3. Site Visit: When possible, I visit the property in person to walk the land, photograph key areas, and study how light and texture define its character. These impressions guide every brushstroke that follows.
  4. Design & Illustration: The map is sketched, refined, and hand-painted with both accuracy and artistry.
  5. Delivery & Display: Final maps are provided as high-resolution digital files and professional prints for framing or gifting.

From Landscape to Artwork

Western Colorado Ranch Map - Private Client 2025

In the spring of 2025, I was commissioned by a private ranch owner in western Colorado to create a pair of artistic maps that would capture the spirit and sense of place of their property.

Since the land had never been mapped, I visited as soon as the snow melted — walking the roads and trails, noting the light, textures, and subtle contours that make the landscape unique.

The goal was to design maps that would help guests to orient themselves to the surrounding landscape, and as a centerpiece for the family’s home at the ranch: a lasting portrait of the land they love. Using a high-resolution 3D terrain model, field sketches, and hundreds of reference photos, I hand-sketched and painted each map, blending geographic accuracy with artistic detail. Every pasture, ridgeline, and grove of trees was carefully painted to reflect the character and rhythm of the land itself.

The first map takes an aerial perspective, looking up the valley across the full expanse of the ranch. I subtly stretched the landscape so that the distant view matches the exact vista seen from the main living room window—blending realism with the way the land feels when you’re there.

The second map takes a traditional top-down perspective, offering a comprehensive view of the entire ranch and surrounding terrain. Designed to highlight the connectivity of the landscape, it weaves together roads, rivers, and ridgelines into a flowing composition that feels both geographic and alive.

Favorite hunting and fishing areas are subtly marked, while local wildlife—from elk and mountain lions to trout and black bears—are brought to life through watercolor illustration.

This map captures not only the structure of the land but its spirit: the movement of animals, the rhythm of the seasons, and the family’s deep relationship with their property.
